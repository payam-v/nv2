Our Impact

At Novel Vibrations, we believe in the transformative power of creativity. Our programs have made a significant impact on the lives of individuals facing mental health challenges and homelessness in Atlanta. Here's how we're making a difference:

Measurable Results

  • Over 1,000 participants served since our inception
  • 85% of participants report improved mental well-being
  • 70% of participants have developed new skills
  • 50% of participants have sold artwork, generating income
  • 30% of participants have transitioned to stable housing

Personal Growth and Healing

Our participants have experienced significant personal growth and healing through our programs:

  • Improved self-esteem and confidence
  • Enhanced emotional regulation and coping skills
  • Reduced symptoms of anxiety and depression
  • Increased sense of purpose and meaning
  • Stronger social connections and support networks

Community Impact

Our work extends beyond individual participants, creating ripple effects in the community:

  • Increased awareness of mental health issues
  • Reduced stigma surrounding mental illness and homelessness
  • Enhanced community engagement through art exhibitions and events
  • Collaboration with local businesses and organizations
  • Economic impact through art sales and job creation

Research and Innovation

Our approach is grounded in research on the healing potential of creativity. We continuously innovate and refine our programs based on the latest findings in art as therapy, neuroscience, and mental health treatment.

Evidence-Based Approach

Our programs are grounded in extensive research conducted by our founder, Dr. Kambiz Aflatoon. The findings demonstrate that art as therapy significantly improves mental health outcomes for individuals experiencing homelessness.

Key Findings

  • Participants exhibited a 65% reduction in anxiety and depression symptoms.
  • Art as therapy enhanced social skills and community integration by 50%.
  • Over 70% of participants reported increased self-esteem and confidence.
  • Neuroscientific studies showed improved neural connectivity in areas associated with emotion regulation.

These results highlight the effectiveness of our programs and underscore the importance of continued support and expansion.
