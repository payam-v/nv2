Our Story

Novel Vibrations was founded by Dr. Kambiz Aflatoon, inspired by the success of the "Living Museum" model. This innovative approach demonstrates how creative expression can promote healing and personal growth for individuals facing mental health challenges and homelessness.

Our Vision

We envision a world where every individual, regardless of their mental health status or living situation, has the opportunity to express themselves creatively, develop their skills, and find pathways to healing and self-sufficiency.

Our Impact

We focus our efforts in Atlanta's Little Five Points and Reynoldstown neighborhoods, offering free art as therapy workshops, entrepreneurial training, and opportunities for our participants to showcase and sell their artwork. Through our programs, we've helped numerous individuals:

  • Find purpose and meaning in their lives
  • Develop valuable artistic and business skills
  • Build self-esteem and confidence
  • Connect with others and reduce isolation
  • Generate income through the sale of their artwork
  • Work towards self-sufficiency and improved mental health

Our Approach

At Novel Vibrations, we believe in the power of creativity to heal and transform lives. Our approach is based on several key principles:

  • Non-judgmental acceptance: We create a safe space where individuals can express themselves freely without fear of criticism or judgment.
  • Skill development: We provide training in various artistic techniques and entrepreneurial skills to empower our participants.
  • Community building: We foster a sense of belonging and connection among our participants, staff, and volunteers.
  • Holistic support: We recognize that creativity is just one aspect of healing, and we connect our participants with additional resources for housing, healthcare, and other essential needs.
  • Empowerment: We encourage our participants to take ownership of their creative journey and personal growth.

Through our programs and community partnerships, we strive to make a lasting impact on the lives of those we serve and contribute to a more compassionate and inclusive society.
